About Flare

Flare provide threat intelligence tailored for the gaming industry in a bid to reduce the negative impact cheats have on IP and gamer experiences. Over six years, I’ve provided product design support to Flare as they’ve grown from a small startup with a relatively simple platform, to an industry leader with a client roster that includes the biggest names in games publishing.

My partnership with Flare has been focused on bringing clarity and structure to their product vision, working closely with founders and developers alike.

Out of Your Head

A key early step was to extract the founders' ideas and insights in order to prioritise design actions and communicate scope with the wider team. This involved conducting focused workshops with the founders to ask critical questions, prioritize requirements, define features and rapidly visualise concepts. The output of these sessions included sitemaps, wireframes and lo-fi prototypes, aligning the team on the product's direction.

Crafting Components

With the core features and user journeys defined, my focus shifted to establishing an elegant visual identity and flexible design system capable of scaling as new features were added. This involved defining reusable design elements and patterns that could be applied consistently across the platform. We developed a comprehensive set of components for key interface elements like navigation, buttons, tabs, form fields, and lists. Disciplined and consistent use of type styles, colour palette, and icons created a cohesive and professional look and feel.

Developer Collaboration

Ensuring a smooth transition from design to development was paramount. I created detailed high-fidelity Figma prototypes that served as a north star for developers during the build phase. Regular check-ins and build reviews with front-end developers were conducted to address any implementation questions and ensure the product was being built according to the design specifications. Particular attention was given to creating design for interative states and edge cases in the UX.

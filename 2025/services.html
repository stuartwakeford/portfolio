SERVICES

Process: Whilst every project is different, here’s how I tend to work:

Discovery

This is all about deeply understanding the problem space and the opportunity. I gather and prioritise requirements from stakeholders, conducting thorough market analysis to understand the landscape. Where needed I’ll carry out competitor analysis to identify existing solutions and potential differentiators.

A crucial part of this phase is comprehensive user research – ideally speaking directly with target users to identify their needs, goals, and pain points.

Experience

Once we have a solid understanding from Discovery, we translate insights into a tangible product structure. We define the core feature set required to address the identified needs and then tease out the detail of each feature (considering all possible user flows and edge cases). This leads to defining the information architecture – how content and features are organised and navigated. I then start creating wireframes to outline the layout and functionality of each screen.

User testing is incredibly valuable here, offering a chance to gather feedback and validate decisions with real users before front-end design starts.

Aesthetics

With the user flow and structure established, the Aesthetics phase brings the product to life visually. This involves creating high-fidelity mockups that define the look and feel, through layout, colour, typography, and graphics. For web work I’ll consider layout across different screen sizes; For apps I’ll consider iOS/Android nuances.

Beyond the core product screens, I’ll often develop supporting marketing assets and essential business collateral like investor decks and walkthrough videos, ensuring a consistent visual identity across all touchpoints. I also create guides for animation and interactions.

Collaboration with Dev

I act as a vital bridge between design and development. I believe it’s essential to have engineers involved with all of the above phases of work and regularly check that what we’re designing is technically feasible. Once the design phase is finished I provide engineers with all the necessary specifications, assets, and guidance to ensure design is implemented accurately.

I tend to conduct regular design reviews of released code to provide feedback, catch any discrepancies from the intended design, and work with developers to ensure design standards are delivered.

Strategic

Beyond the craft of UX/UI design, my role is fundamentally about acting as a strategic partner to my clients. Drawing on my experience, I serve as a trusted advisor on critical product decisions. A key part of this involves skilfully balancing business goals with crucial user requirements. I contribute significantly to defining the product scope, prioritising features, and planning releases, providing a clear vision for developers and the wider team.

My strategic input also extends to user acquisition and retention, identifying how design can directly impact these key business drivers and contribute to growth.
